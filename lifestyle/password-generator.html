Why Use Strong Passwords?

Strong passwords are your first line of defense against unauthorized access to your accounts. Weak passwords can be cracked in seconds using modern computing power, while strong passwords can take centuries to break.

⚠️ Password Security Facts

A 6-character password can be cracked in under 1 second. A 16-character password with mixed characters can take centuries to crack using brute force methods.

Password Best Practices

  • Use unique passwords: Never reuse passwords across different accounts
  • Minimum 12 characters: Longer passwords are exponentially more secure
  • Mix character types: Combine uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols
  • Aoid personal information: Don't use birthdays, names, or common words
  • Use a password manager: Store passwords securely instead of memorizing them

Understanding Password Strength

Password strength is measured in "bits of entropy" - a mathematical representation of how random and unpredictable a password is. Higher entropy means better security:

Entropy Strength Crack Time
< 28 bits Very Weak < 1 second
28-35 bits Weak Seconds to minutes
36-59 bits Moderate Hours to days
60-79 bits Strong Years to centuries
80+ bits Very Strong Centuries+
