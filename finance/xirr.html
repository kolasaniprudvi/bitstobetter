The Mechanics Behind Mutual Fund XIRR Calculator

The Extended Internal Rate of Return (XIRR) uses numerical methods (like Newton-Raphson) to solve for the discount rate that makes the Net Present Value (NPV) of all irregular cash flows exactly zero.

0 = ∑ [Cash Flow / (1 + XIRR)^((Date - StartDate)/365)]
2

Strategic Execution

XIRR is the only mathematically accurate way to measure the performance of a Mutual Fund portfolio with irregular SIPs, lumpsums, and withdrawals.

Key Parameters

  • ● Benchmarking: Always compare your portfolio XIRR against a benchmark index (like Nifty 50) to see if your active stock picking is actually adding value.
  • ● Tax Drag: Calculate post-tax XIRR by inputting your tax payments as negative cash flows to understand your true wealth generation.

Myth vs. Fact

The Myth

"Absolute return (Profit / Investment) is a good way to measure Mutual Fund performance."

The Fact

Absolute return ignores time. A 50% absolute return over 10 years is terrible (4.1% XIRR), but 50% over 2 years is incredible (22.4% XIRR).

Why use XIRR over CAGR?

Unlike CAGR (Compound Annual Growth Rate) which assumes a single initial investment growing over time, XIRR (Extended Internal Rate of Return) accounts for multiple investments and withdrawals happening at irregular intervals.

This makes it the only accurate way to calculate the true annualized return of a Mutual Fund SIP, stock portfolio, or any investment journey where you continuously add or remove funds over time.
