    The Math Behind Financial Independence

    At its core, FIRE accumulation rests on a simple formula: Corpus = Annual Expenses in Retirement ÷ Safe Withdrawal Rate (SWR). But the expenses you have today won't be the same in 15 years because of inflation.

    Myth vs. Scientific Fact

    The Myth

    "I just need ₹1 Crore (or $1 Million) to retire and live off the interest forever without worrying."

    The Scientific Fact

    Due to inflation (purchasing power erosion), a fixed corpus loses value. Safe withdrawal research (like the Trinity Study) proves you must adjust your target based on projected spending at the exact year you retire.

    Spending at independence is today’s baseline grown by inflation: E_T = E_0 × (1 + i)^T. Our FIRE Engine calculates the required monthly investments as an annuity-due (meaning you invest at the start of the month, not the end).

    Key Takeaways for Your Plan:

    • Inflation is the silent tax: Always account for a minimum 6% inflation in India (or 3-4% globally).
    • The 4% Rule is outdated: Many modern planners lean towards a more conservative 3.25% - 3.5% SWR to survive extended market downturns.
    • Start Early: Compound interest does the heavy lifting in the final years of accumulation.
