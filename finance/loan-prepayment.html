Loan Pre-payment Benefits

Pre-paying your loan reduces the outstanding principal, which reduces the interest component. You can choose to either reduce your EMI amount or shorten the loan tenure.

Reduce Tenure vs Reduce EMI

  • Reduce Tenure: Higher interest savings, loan closes earlier
  • Reduce EMI: Lower monthly burden, more cash flow flexibility

The Mechanics Behind Loan Pre-payment: Free Investment & Loan Planning Tool

Loan prepayment math exploits the reducing-balance amortization curve. By directly injecting capital into the principal early in the loan, you instantly vaporize years of compounding interest scheduled on that specific chunk of money.

New Balance = Old Balance - Prepayment
2

Strategic Execution

Prepaying is mathematically identical to investing your money at a guaranteed, risk-free rate equal to your loan interest rate.

Key Parameters

  • ● Early Execution: A $5,000 prepayment in Year 1 destroys significantly more interest than a $5,000 prepayment in Year 15.
  • ● Tax Arbitrage: If your home loan interest is tax-deductible, calculate your *effective* after-tax loan rate before choosing to prepay versus invest in the market.

Myth vs. Fact

The Myth

"Prepaying a little bit every month doesn't make a big difference."

The Fact

Consistently paying just 10% extra towards your principal every month can shave years off a 30-year mortgage and save tens of thousands in interest.
