What is CAGR?

Compound Annual Growth Rate (CAGR) is the annual growth rate of an investment over a specified period of time, assuming the profits are reinvested at the end of each period.

CAGR Formula

CAGR = [(Ending Value / Starting Value)^(1/n)] - 1

Where n = number of years

Why CAGR Matters

CAGR smooths out volatility and provides a single annual growth rate, making it easier to compare different investments over different time periods.

Key Parameters

  • ● Data Normalization: Use CAGR to compare a volatile stock portfolio against a stable fixed deposit on an apples-to-apples basis.
  • ● Real Returns: Always subtract inflation from your nominal CAGR to understand your true wealth generation.

Myth vs. Fact

The Myth

"Average annual return is the same as CAGR."

The Fact

Average return ignores the geometric realities of compounding. If you lose 50% one year and gain 50% the next, your average is 0%, but your actual CAGR is deeply negative.
