The Mechanics Behind Home Loan Eligibility: Free Investment & Loan Planning Tool

Home loan eligibility is dictated by Fixed Obligation to Income Ratio (FOIR). Banks run algorithms to ensure your total monthly EMI outflows do not breach a specific threshold of your net income.

Max EMI = Net Monthly Income × FOIR Limit (e.g. 50%)

Strategic Execution

Maximizing your eligibility involves aggressive debt structuring in the months prior to your mortgage application.

Key Parameters

  • ● Clear Short-term Debt: Paying off small personal loans or car loans dramatically reduces your existing FOIR, unlocking massive mortgage eligibility.
  • ● Co-applicants: Adding an earning spouse as a co-applicant aggregates both net incomes, proportionally expanding the FOIR ceiling.

Myth vs. Fact

The Myth

"My eligibility is purely based on my credit score."

The Fact

Your credit score gets you the loan at a good rate, but your FOIR (income vs existing debt) dictates exactly how much capital the bank will risk on you.
