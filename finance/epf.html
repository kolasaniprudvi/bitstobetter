Employee Provident Fund (EPF)

EPF is a government-backed retirement savings scheme where both employee and employer contribute 12% of basic salary each month. The current EPFO interest rate is 8.25%.

EPF Contribution Structure

  • Employee: 12% of basic salary goes to EPF
  • Employer: 12% of basic salary (3.67% to EPF + 8.33% to EPS)
  • Total: 15.67% of basic salary accumulates in EPF

The Mechanics Behind EPF Calculator: Free Investment & Loan Planning Tool

The Employees' Provident Fund (EPF) utilizes annual compounding on monthly contributions. Both employee and employer contributions are tallied monthly, but the interest is mathematically applied at the financial year-end.

Interest = Monthly Running Balance × (Rate / 12)

Strategic Execution

EPF is an Exempt-Exempt-Exempt (EEE) asset, making it one of the most powerful tax-free wealth generators available to salaried individuals.

Key Parameters

  • ● Voluntary Provident Fund (VPF): You can boost your EPF corpus by voluntarily contributing beyond the mandatory 12%, earning the same high tax-free rate.
  • ● Withdrawal Penalties: Avoid withdrawing EPF before 5 years of continuous service to prevent heavy taxation on the accumulated corpus.

Myth vs. Fact

The Myth

"I should withdraw my EPF whenever I switch jobs."

The Fact

Transferring your EPF instead of withdrawing it preserves the massive compounding momentum you have already built.
