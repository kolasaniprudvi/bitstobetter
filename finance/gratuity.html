Gratuity Calculation Formula

Gratuity is calculated based on your last drawn salary and years of service:

Under Payment of Gratuity Act

Gratuity = (Last Drawn Salary × 15 × Years of Service) / 26

Not Covered under Act

Gratuity = (Last Drawn Salary × 15 × Years of Service) / 30

Tax Exemption

Gratuity is tax-free up to ₹20 lakh under Section 10(10) of the Income Tax Act. Amount above ₹20 lakh is taxable.

Strategic Execution

Gratuity acts as a forced loyalty bonus. Understanding the tenure requirements is crucial before making career transitions.

Key Parameters

  • ● The 5-Year Rule: In most jurisdictions, gratuity only kicks in after exactly 4 years, 10 months, and 11 days of continuous service.
  • ● Salary Components: Only Basic + DA are considered. Massive allowances in your CTC do not inflate your gratuity payout.

Myth vs. Fact

The Myth

"My entire CTC is used to calculate my gratuity payout."

The Fact

Only your Basic Pay and Dearness Allowance are factored into the statutory formula, which is why employers often heavily weight allowances in the CTC.
