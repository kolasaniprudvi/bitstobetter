GST Calculation Explained

GST (Goods and Services Tax) is a single indirect tax on the supply of goods and services. GST is divided into CGST (Central GST), SGST (State GST), and IGST (Integrated GST) for interstate transactions.

GST Slabs in India

  • 5% - Essential goods, household items
  • 12% - Processed food, restaurants
  • 18% - Most goods and services
  • 28% - Luxury items, automobiles

The Mechanics Behind GST Calculator: Free Investment & Loan Planning Tool

Goods and Services Tax (GST) math is a simple percentage application, but it varies whether the tax is exclusive (added on top) or inclusive (baked into the final price).

Exclusive: Price × (1 + GST%) | Inclusive: Price / (1 + GST%)

Strategic Execution

For businesses, managing Input Tax Credit (ITC) is where the true strategic value of GST math lies, ensuring you only pay tax on the value added.

Key Parameters

  • ● Forward vs Reverse Charge: Understand when you are liable to pay GST directly to the government versus paying it to your supplier.
  • ● Pricing Strategy: When setting consumer prices, using the inclusive formula ensures your final display price hits psychological thresholds (like $99).

Myth vs. Fact

The Myth

"If GST is 18%, I just subtract 18% from the final price to get the base price."

The Fact

Mathematically, subtracting 18% from a post-tax amount yields a different number than dividing by 1.18. The inclusive formula must be used.
