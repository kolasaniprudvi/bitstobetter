The Mechanics Behind Public Provident Fund (PPF) Calculator

The Public Provident Fund (PPF) is a sovereign-backed, tax-free savings vehicle in India. Because it falls under the "Exempt-Exempt-Exempt" (EEE) category, your deposits, accrued interest, and final maturity amount are entirely tax-free.

Myth vs. Scientific Fact

The Myth

"I should just invest at the end of the financial year to save tax."

The Scientific Fact

PPF interest is calculated on the minimum balance between the 5th and the end of each month. If you invest after the 5th, you lose interest for that entire month. Investing a lumpsum between April 1st and April 5th maximizes your annual returns.

3

Strategic Execution

PPF is the gold standard for risk-free, tax-free debt allocation in an Indian portfolio.

Key Parameters

  • ● Timing Matters: To mathematically maximize your PPF returns, deposit the full ₹1.5 Lakh lumpsum before April 5th every financial year.
  • ● The 15-Year Lock-in: Use the 15-year lock-in as a feature, not a bug, to prevent behavioral panic selling of your debt allocation.

Myth vs. Fact

The Myth

"I should only open a PPF account when I am older and closer to retirement."

The Fact

Opening a PPF account early is critical because you can extend it in blocks of 5 years indefinitely after maturity, creating a highly liquid, tax-free compounding machine.
