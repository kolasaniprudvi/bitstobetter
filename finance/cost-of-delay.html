The Mechanics Behind the Cost of Delay

A Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) relies on mathematical compounding—earning returns on your previously earned returns. Unlike simple interest, an SIP calculates the future value of a series of cash flows using the following formula:

FV = P × [((1 + r)n - 1) / r] × (1 + r)

Variables Explained

  • ● P: Monthly Investment Amount.
  • ● r: Monthly Return Rate (Annual Rate ÷ 12).
  • ● n: Total Number of Months.

Rupee Cost Averaging

In falling markets, your fixed amount buys more units at a lower Net Asset Value (NAV). In rising markets, you buy fewer units. This averages out the cost of your investments and heavily mitigates market timing risk.

SIP vs Lumpsum Execution

Many investors debate whether to deploy a large sum at once or break it into monthly investments. Here is the objective difference:

Aspect SIP Lumpsum
Cash Flow Regular fractional deployments One-time massive deployment
Market Timing Mitigates timing risk High risk of buying at peak
Psychology Automates discipline Requires emotional control during dips

The Delay Cost

Waiting for the "perfect time" to invest is the most expensive mistake you can make. A ₹10,000 monthly SIP at 12% for 30 years grows to ₹3.5 Crore. If you delay starting by just 5 years, your final corpus drops to ₹1.9 Crore. You lose ₹1.6 Crore just by waiting 5 years.
